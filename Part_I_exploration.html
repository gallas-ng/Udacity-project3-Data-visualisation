Issues¶

Quality

  • start_time, end_time are object should be datetime
  • start_station_id, end_station_id are float should be str
  • bike_id is int should be str
  • member_birth_year is float should be int
  • user_type, member_gender should be categorical types
  • nan rows for stations should be deleted

  • rows where calculated 'age' is more than 90 should be deleted

    Tidyness

  • age field calculated on member_birth_year should be added
  • start_day and end_day

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

There are 183,138 bikes sharing entries from February 1st and 28th 2019 which records.

  • Duration of the trip in seconds
  • Start and End timestamps (date/hour)
  • Start and End Stations names, gps coordinates and id
  • Bike id number
  • Type of user (whether the user is a subscriber or customer)
  • Various personal data (Gender / Birth Year)
  • Bike share for all trip which is an affordable pricing plan option
  • Age (added column) of user
  • Start and End days (added columns)

    Most variables are numeric in nature, but the variables user_type, member_gender, and Bike_share_for_all_trip are categorical variables.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I'm most interested in figuring out how the duration is affected by other variables.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

I expect that weekdays, member_gender, user_type, age will have main effects on the duration. I also think that the hours, will have effects on duration.

Conclusions¶

Given the results of our analyses we were able to conclude:

  • the duration of the trips is longer during the weekends
  • the majority of Go users are subscribers but the most trips are made by customers who apparently often make trips at night

  • and the gender of members does not really affect the duration
